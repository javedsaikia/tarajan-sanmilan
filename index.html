Our Story · আমাৰ কাহিনী

A Century of
Community & Pride

Tarajan is a locality at the heart of Jorhat city. This name is mentioned in the Tungkhungia Chronicles. The name Tarajan comes from the name of the rivulet that flows through the area. There were many black galangal plants (in Assamese, Taragosh) on both sides of the rivulet at that time, and hence the name of the rivulet became Tarajan — 'Jan' being the Assamese term for a rivulet. In the Tarajan locality, on an August day of 1918, a club named Asomiya Lorar Charitra Gothon Sabha was established. This was the first public organization in the Tarajan area, which is now extinct. This club helped organize the youth of the area to advance in sports. Under the banner of this club, in 1927, a new organization — the Tarajan Sanmilan — was established. In Tarajan, Durga Puja had been celebrated prior to the formation of Tarajan Sanmilan, but in a casual manner. After the formation of Tarajan Sanmilan it became regular under its aegis. Simultaneously, the Tarajan Sanmilan also started activities for the cultural upbringing of the locality. During the Puja days, in the first year of its establishment, two plays were staged — Jahnnavi and Rana Pratap Singh.

At that time, the Tarajan Sanmilan had no land of its own. Later, the late Bipin Chandra Bardoloi, EAC of Jorhat, allocated the necessary government land for the Tarajan Sanmilan. He was employed in Jorhat from 1912 to 1929, and during that time, through the efforts of various others including him, the Tarajan Sanmilan gained a limited permanent form with wooden and iron poles. Many of the people who led the Tarajan Sanmilan in the early years were pioneers in various fields of Jorhat and Assam. Among these were Advocate late Purnananda Sharma Pathak and Advocate late Jadunath Barua — these two held important portfolios like President and Vice-President of the Jorhat Municipal Board during the then British rule. This list also includes late Radhanath Barthakur, who was the founder of the prestigious Happy Lodge hotels in Shillong and Guwahati. Similarly, late Umacharan Baruah was a prominent tea planter.

Apart from theatre performances and Durga Puja celebrations, the Tarajan Sanmilan has been doing much work for the social and spiritual upliftment of the area. The late Samar Bordoloi, one of the pioneer body builders of Assam, inspired and trained a number of teenagers and youths at the Tarajan Sanmilan premises in the 1980s.

"Where community gathers, culture lives on."

100 Years · ১০০ বছৰ

A Century of Milestones

From humble beginnings in 1927 to a vibrant centenary, each decade has written its own chapter.

1927
Foundation

Tarajan Sanmilan is established in the Tarajan locality of Jorhat, Assam.

1928
The First Stage

Bipin Bardoloi and Indreswar Saikia gather four iron posts and a few wooden posts to raise the Sanmilan's first temporary stage.

1960
The Constitution

The Sanmilan's formal rules are framed in 1960, and later amended in 1978 and 2001.

1978
Three Committees

From 1927 to 1978 the Sanmilan ran under a single committee. In 1978 it is reorganised into three wings — the Central Committee, the Puja Committee and the Cultural Committee.

1996
Emblem & Flag

The Sanmilan adopts an official emblem and a flag bearing that emblem.

2001
Platinum Jubilee

The Sanmilan celebrates its Platinum Jubilee. Its commemorative souvenir চয়নিকা (Choynika), edited by the late Mahesh Chandra Bhattacharya, becomes the Sanmilan's first published document.

2007
The 'Tarajan' Journal

The Sanmilan's annual journal 'Tarajan' is published for the first time.

2012
Women's Forum

The Tarajan Mahila Chora (Women's Forum) is founded on 3 June 2012.

2018–19
A New Home

Construction of the Sanmilan's new premises begins under a new project.

2027
Centenary — 100 Years

A year-long celebration marks a century of service, culture and community. The journey continues into the next hundred years.

Early Years · আৰম্ভণিৰ পৰম্পৰা

Traditions of the Formative Years

Worship & the Idols

In the earliest years, worship was offered before a sacred ghat rather than an idol. Later, idols crafted by the father of the late Makhan Sharma of Tarajan Brahmin village were used; from 1960, idols made by professional artists were introduced.

Painted Backdrops & 'Siraj'

Early plays used no modern stagecraft; painted backdrops of palaces, forests and more were hung to evoke each setting. They were painted by Lakshiram Baruah, who studied fine art in Calcutta and served as drawing master at Jorhat Government School. The first play staged with such scenery was 'Siraj', conceived by Nab Baruah.

The First Actress — 'Fulor Mel'

The first woman to perform on the Sanmilan stage appeared in the dance-drama 'Fulor Mel', written, composed and directed by Deven Chakravarty. Until then, men had played the women's roles.

A Permanent Auditorium

During Purnananda Sharma Pathak's tenure as secretary, the auditorium was built in permanent form with brick and lime.

The Puja Ghar

While Radhanath Barthakur was president, he had the puja house built permanently at his own expense; its front portion was later added by the Sanmilan's puja branch through public donations.
